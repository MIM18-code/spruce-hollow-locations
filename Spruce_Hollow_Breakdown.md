## Story Summary

Four college-aged friends drive to an abandoned juvenile detention facility in Western Maryland to investigate abuse that occurred there decades ago. One (Caleb) is the son of a former inmate. They become trapped by the facility's supernatural binding, forced into its schedule of bells, meals, and confessions. By the end, two are dead, two are absorbed, and the cycle begins again.

**Genre:** Horror / Drama
**Period:** Present day (2024), with archival footage from 1947-1994
**Tone:** Institutional horror, character-driven, slow burn escalation

## Scheduling Notes

### Location Grouping (Efficiency)
The majority of the film takes place at one compound location (Spruce Hollow). Sub-locations within the compound:

1. **MAIN YARD (EXT)** - 15+ scenes, all leads, most of shoot. Central staging area.
2. **MESS HALL (INT)** - 5 scenes. Group all mess hall work (ghost children needed).
3. **ISOLATION BLOCK / CELLS (INT)** - 10 scenes. Heavy SFX/VFX days.
4. **ADMIN BUILDING (INT)** - Records Room, Assembly Hall, Director's Office, Hallways - 15 scenes combined.
5. **DORMITORY (INT)** - 5 scenes. Room 12 and Room 37.
6. **BOILER ROOM / TUNNEL / SHED (INT)** - 3 scenes. Build as connected set.

### Recommended Shoot Order
1. **Block 1 (5 days):** Elena's Apartment (INT/EXT) + Subaru driving scenes (day) + Gas Station + Route 68 - all DAY work, no VFX
2. **Block 2 (8 days):** Spruce Hollow DAY exteriors (Main Yard arrivals, exploration, group session yard)
3. **Block 3 (12 days):** Spruce Hollow DAY interiors (Admin, Records, Assembly Hall, Mess Hall, Isolation first visits, Director's Office)
4. **Block 4 (15 days):** Spruce Hollow NIGHT interiors (Dormitory Room 12, Gymnasium, Cells, Mess Hall night, Director's Office intimacy scene, Boiler/Tunnel/Shed, Maintenance Shed confrontation)
5. **Block 5 (10 days):** Spruce Hollow NIGHT exteriors (Main Yard night, group session confessions, Fischer pursuit, woods escape)
6. **Block 6 (6 days):** Subaru NIGHT interiors (trapped in car sequences)
7. **Block 7 (3 days):** Motel scenes, final twist, epilogue
8. **Block 8 (2 days):** Archival footage (16mm, VHS, camcorder - can be second unit)

### Night Shoot Grouping
- Group all night work in Blocks 4-6 (~31 nights)
- Consider flipping schedule for continuous night block to avoid day/night alternation
- Night exteriors (woods, yard) weather-dependent

### Actor Availability Clusters
- **Elena (all 61 days):** In virtually every scene. Cannot be released early.
- **Sonal (est. 50 days):** Can be released after woods/escape scenes shot. Compound fracture prosthetic limits mobility - schedule her heavy VFX/makeup scenes consecutively.
- **Jordan (est. 48 days):** Camera always present. Can be released after woods scenes.
- **Caleb (est. 45 days):** Can be released after Maintenance Shed death scene. His counselor ghost appearance (final scene) can be picked up separately.
- **Fischer (est. 12 days):** Can be grouped. All Fischer scenes are high-intensity. Cluster his work into 2 weeks.
- **Tommy (est. 8 days):** All at Spruce Hollow compound. Group with mess hall and group session days.
- **Clerk/Earl (1 day):** Gas station only.
- **Ghost Children (est. 12 days):** Cluster with mess hall, assembly hall, group session, and yard scenes. Child labor laws: limited hours.
- **William (1-2 days):** Vision/flashback inserts only. Can be picked up during Isolation Block days.

### Weather / Conditions
- Script is set in November, Western Maryland. Cold, overcast, bare trees.
- Exteriors need consistent late-autumn look. Avoid green foliage.
- Dawn scenes (2-3) require magic hour scheduling.
- Rain not scripted but would enhance atmosphere.

### Child Actor Considerations
- 4-6 child actors needed across ~12 shooting days
- SAG rules for minors: limited work hours (8-10 hrs depending on age), studio teacher required
- Coordinated movement scenes (unison eating, reciting, screaming) need rehearsal time
- Some disturbing content in child scenes - welfare coordinator required
