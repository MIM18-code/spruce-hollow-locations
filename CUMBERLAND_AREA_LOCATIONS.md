# CUMBERLAND AREA LOCATIONS — SPRUCE HOLLOW SUPPLEMENTARY SCOUT

**Prepared:** April 16, 2026
**For:** Joshua Land / MindInMotion
**Scope:** Allegany County MD (primary), Garrett County MD, Mineral/Hampshire WV, Somerset/Bedford PA — supplementary to Trans-Allegheny (Weston, WV) or Pennhurst (Spring City, PA) anchor locations. Proximity anchor: Green Ridge State Forest.

---

## TL;DR — WHAT THIS REGION ACTUALLY OFFERS

Cumberland is a declining-industrial small city (~18k) that lost two hospitals in 2009, a 131-year paper mill in 2019, and a 1926 high school in 2022. Most of the headline "dead" sites were already demolished by the time you'd call. The remaining shootable inventory is:

1. **Repurposed industrial interiors** (Footer Building, Canal Place) — brick, tile, period scale, but occupied / commercial
2. **Operating historic civic buildings** (Masonic Temple, Emmanuel Episcopal, Allegany Museum, Cumberland Armory/Windsor Castle) — rentable, atmospheric, but **not** abandoned
3. **Railroad infrastructure** (WMSR shops in Ridgeley WV, 1913 station, Frostburg 1891 depot) — owned by WMSR, period stock
4. **Green Ridge State Forest + Green Ridge Youth Center** — already anchored; the youth center is literally a juvenile facility 30 minutes from Cumberland

**There is no operational abandoned hospital/asylum/school in the Cumberland area as of April 2026.** Memorial, Sacred Heart/Braddock, and old Allegany High are all demolished. The closest "abandoned institutional" shell play is Trans-Allegheny itself (53 miles SW) or Pennhurst (174 miles NE). What Cumberland provides is **atmospheric exteriors, period streetscapes, small-scene interiors, and a forest anchor** — not a substitute for the primary facility.

**Crew base verdict:** No meaningful local crew. Nearest substantive grip/lighting is Pittsburgh (2hr), DC/Baltimore (2.5hr), or Morgantown WV for smaller units. Import crew + rental trucks — budget for travel days and housing.

## RANKED CANDIDATE SITES

### 1. GREEN RIDGE YOUTH CENTER (Flintstone, MD)
**Address:** 28600 Headquarters Dr NE, Flintstone, MD 21530 (inside Green Ridge State Forest)
**Status:** ACTIVE state juvenile facility — Maryland Department of Juvenile Services. 30-bed all-male treatment program. **Scheduled for closure in fiscal year 2027** per 2021 DJS consolidation plan.
**Why it matters for Spruce Hollow:** This is *literally the thing the script depicts*, operating inside your already-planned forest anchor. If the closure proceeds on schedule, the site will transition in 2026–2027 and *could* be filmable post-decommission through the state.
**Doubles for:** The facility itself — exterior establishing, wood-frame dorms, admin buildings, forestry-camp aesthetic. Originally opened 1955 as "Green Ridge Forestry Camp for Boys."
**Owner / contact path:** Maryland Department of Juvenile Services + Maryland Department of Natural Resources (forest jurisdiction). Start via Maryland Film Office (Catherine Batavick, Acting Director, catherine.batavick@maryland.gov, 410-767-6340) — MFO has the inside track on state-owned facility access. Secondary: DJS Office of Communications.
**Dealbreakers:** Currently operating with youth in custody. Active assault/safety issues reported in 2024 JJMU report — this is a live facility with real kids. Zero chance of access while occupied. Only viable if production timeline aligns with 2026–2027 closure.

**Action:** Email MFO now to flag interest in the site post-decommission. This is the single highest-value lead in the region.

### 2. TRANS-ALLEGHENY LUNATIC ASYLUM (Weston, WV) — REFERENCE BENCHMARK
**Address:** 71 Asylum Dr, Weston, WV 26452
**Distance from Cumberland:** 53 miles / ~1hr 15min via US-50 W
**Status:** Private owner (Joe Jordan, purchased 2007 at auction). Operates as paid tours + overnight ghost hunts + film location rentals. Film-friendly with established workflow.
**Why it's the anchor:** This is the only truly shootable massive institutional shell within 2 hours of Cumberland. It's closer to Cumberland than to most PA/MD production centers — a Cumberland-based unit can day-play there.
**Owner / contact path:** trans-alleghenylunaticasylum.com — they have a published rental process. Expect 4-figure daily location fees for feature film use.
**Dealbreakers:** Heavy prior film/TV usage means look is not exclusive (Buzzfeed, Ghost Adventures, indie horror have shot there). Schedule competition with paid tour operations.

---

### 3. FOOTER BUILDING / FORMER FOOTER'S DYE WORKS (Cumberland, MD)
**Address:** 2 Howard St, Cumberland, MD 21502 (Canal Place)
**Status:** Renovated 2018. Mixed-use: 24 luxury loft apartments + commercial (Dig Deep Brewing, restaurant). National Register of Historic Places (2013). Four stories, 1906 brick with original terra cotta tile roof.
**Doubles for:** Period industrial interiors — boiler rooms, factory floors, steel-sash windows, freight elevators, raw brick corridors. The common areas and un-renovated back-of-house spaces retain dye works character. Could sub for facility boiler room, industrial laundry (juvenile detention laundries are a real set piece), utility tunnels.
**Owner / contact path:** Footer Building Development LLC via footerbuilding.com. No published filming contact — approach through the leasing office. Dig Deep Brewing has done events; owner is sympathetic to creative use.
**Dealbreakers:** Occupied residential building — noise/access management for tenants. Most photogenic raw-industrial character has been preserved but softened by residential conversion. The *best* spaces are already apartments.

---

### 4. WESTERN MARYLAND SCENIC RAILROAD — RIDGELEY SHOPS & STATIONS
**Addresses:**
- WMSR Cumberland Station: 13 Canal St, Cumberland, MD (1913 WM Railway station, restored)
- Frostburg Depot: 18 Depot Rd, Frostburg, MD (1891 C&P Railroad wooden depot)
- **Ridgeley Shops:** Ridgeley, WV (across Potomac from Cumberland) — old WM Railway locomotive shops, now WMSR operating base
**Status:** All owned/operated by WMSR (Scenic Railroad Development Corp). Active tourist railroad with 2-1309 steam locomotive. Shops are working but retain 1913 character.
**Doubles for:** Backstory/flashback exteriors (rail town arrival, industrial-era Appalachia), transition B-roll, boiler-room/mechanical-shop interiors in Ridgeley. Frostburg wooden depot is remote and atmospheric.
**Owner / contact path:** Western Maryland Scenic Railroad, wmsr.com — they have a development director and are revenue-hungry post-COVID. Film rental is plausible. Steam locomotive shoot would be a serious budget line.
**Dealbreakers:** Tourist schedule conflicts (weekend shoot windows tight during Apr–Oct operating season). Steam operation adds six figures fast; static shots are cheap.

### 5. CUMBERLAND MASONIC TEMPLE (Cumberland, MD)
**Address:** Intersection of Greene and Washington streets, Cumberland, MD (in Washington Street Historic District, adjacent to Emmanuel Episcopal)
**Status:** ACTIVE Masonic lodge, dedicated 1912, designed by Wright Butler. Restoration ongoing via Cumberland Masonic Temple Inc. Roof replaced; main lodge room and upper reception rooms restored. National Treasure: Book of Secrets scouted the building.
**Doubles for:** Institutional formal spaces — hearing rooms, disciplinary chambers, flashback establishments, any "authority" interior with gravitas. The ritualistic Masonic iconography can be dressed out or leaned into for supernatural horror.
**Owner / contact path:** Cumberland Masonic Temple, Inc. — approach via Facebook page (facebook.com/cumberlandmasonictemple) or Allegany Arts Council, which has working relationships with historic property stewards in town. No public booking URL.
**Dealbreakers:** Active lodge — scheduling must work around meetings. Masonic iconography is load-bearing on the walls; expect restrictions on alterations/set dressing that cover Masonic symbols.

### 6. EMMANUEL EPISCOPAL CHURCH (Cumberland, MD)
**Address:** 16 Washington St, Cumberland, MD 21502
**Status:** Active parish. 1851 sandstone Gothic Revival. Built on Fort Cumberland foundations. Tiffany stained glass (three periods). **Documented tunnel network** beneath the church — originally Fort Cumberland walls, later Underground Railroad.
**Doubles for:** Exterior atmospheric, cemetery/churchyard, interior sanctuary for flashback/baptism/funeral, **the tunnels are extraordinary** and the most underutilized horror asset in the region.
**Owner / contact path:** emmanuelparishofmd.org. Parish runs docent tours; rector or parish administrator handles outside requests. A well-pitched indie horror needs to frame this carefully — active Episcopal parish, living congregation.
**Dealbreakers:** Sunday services + weddings + funerals. Tunnel access likely requires safety assessment. Content restrictions if pitch leans too demonic/sacrilegious.

### 7. ALLEGANY MUSEUM (Former U.S. Courthouse & Post Office) (Cumberland, MD)
**Address:** 3 Pershing St, Cumberland, MD 21502
**Status:** Museum occupying 1932 Art Deco/Neoclassical former federal building. Designed by R. Holt Hitchins. Arthur & Bernice Friedland Grand Courtroom is available for rental (events/receptions).
**Doubles for:** Courtroom/hearing scenes, 1930s-period institutional interiors, marble corridors, judge's chambers. Strong for backstory — juvenile court hearing, sentencing flashback.
**Owner / contact path:** Allegany Museum — manager@alleganymuseum.org. Museum is small, under-resourced, receptive to rental income. Friedland Courtroom has published rental pathway.
**Dealbreakers:** Museum operating hours and exhibit protection. Smaller footprint than period courthouses in larger cities. May not support large crew footprint.

---

### 8. CUMBERLAND ARMORY / WINDSOR CASTLE EVENTS CENTRE (Cumberland, MD)
**Address:** 210 South Centre St, Cumberland, MD 21502
**Status:** 1914 Maryland National Guard Armory. Transferred to private use; operates as Windsor Castle Events Centre — largest event center in Allegany County.
**Doubles for:** Large institutional interior volume, drill hall as gymnasium / assembly / dining hall / indoor rec space. Castellated facade reads "fortress/institution."
**Owner / contact path:** Windsor Castle Events Centre — they have published event rental workflows.
**Dealbreakers:** Wedding/event bookings dominate weekends. Converted venue — the drill hall retains scale but has event-venue dressing. Check how much original raw character remains.

---

### 9. MOUNT SAVAGE CASTLE / THE CASTLE B&B (Mt. Savage, MD)
**Address:** 15925 Mt. Savage Rd NW, Mt. Savage, MD 21545
**Status:** Active B&B. 1840 stone house converted ~1890s by Scottish immigrant Andrew Ramsey into replica of Craig Castle. 1984 B&B conversion. Six guest rooms + caretaker cottage.
**Doubles for:** Aristocratic manse / old-money family home / gothic exterior establishing shot. Backstory flashback — "the estate" / ancestor's home / boarding school exterior. Not a facility double; it's a character location.
**Owner / contact path:** The Castle, 301-264-4645, castlebandb.com. Listed for sale in the past (Alex Cooper real estate listing exists) — current operating status should be verified before shoot.
**Dealbreakers:** Operating B&B — cannot shoot with guests in residence. Small. Rural location 15 minutes from Cumberland.

### 10. CUMBERLAND HISTORIC DISTRICT — STREET-LEVEL B-ROLL
**Scope:** Baltimore Street pedestrian mall, Washington Street Historic District, downtown blocks near Canal Place.
**Status:** Operational downtown. 350+ properties on National Register across Cumberland and Frostburg combined.
**Doubles for:** Period exterior driving/walking shots, declining-rust-belt-main-street atmosphere, establishing "small town where something is wrong," arrival/departure sequences.
**Owner / contact path:** City of Cumberland Community Development, 57 N Liberty St, 301-722-2000 x5600. For film permits, start there and loop in Maryland Film Office. Canal Place is managed by Canal Place Preservation & Development Authority.
**Dealbreakers:** Active commerce. Weekend markets. Parking/street-closure permits required.

## SITES CONFIRMED DEMOLISHED OR UNUSABLE (do not pursue)

- **Old Memorial Hospital** (600 Memorial Ave, Cumberland) — demolished 2014. Site owned by Cumberland Economic Development Corp., under stalled development contract with RAZ Development (Bethesda) for 250-unit apartment project. Nothing to shoot.
- **Sacred Heart Hospital / WMHS Braddock Campus** (900/921 Seton Dr, Cumberland) — demolished by 2015. City of Cumberland acquired via land swap with WMHS in 2011 for new school/athletic site. Nothing to shoot.
- **Old Allegany High School** (Sedgwick St, Cumberland) — demolished May 2022. D.R. Horton 65-unit housing development approved. Nothing to shoot.
- **Kelly-Springfield Tire Plant** (701 Kelly Rd, Cumberland) — manufacturing buildings demolished. Site subdivided, partially occupied by Allegany County DPW and small businesses. EPA corrective-action-complete. No shootable factory shell.
- **Luke Paper Mill** (Luke, MD) — 55-acre demolition began August 2023, substantially complete by summer 2024. Some ancillary structures may remain; confirm with Port River West LLC / Allegany County before scouting. Environmental liability concerns (mercury, black liquor residue).
- **Queen City Hotel / B&O Station** (Cumberland) — demolished 1972 for post office. Only post office stands now.
- **Allegany Ballistics Laboratory (Rocket Center, WV)** — active Northrop Grumman munitions facility. Zero film access.
- **FCI Cumberland / Western Correctional Institution** — active federal and state prisons. Zero film access.

## LOCAL FILM SUPPORT INFRASTRUCTURE — ASSESSMENT

### Film Commissions / Permits
- **Maryland Film Office** — the only real state-level resource. Catherine Batavick, Acting Director, catherine.batavick@maryland.gov, 410-767-6340. Karen Glenn Hood for press, karen.glennhood@maryland.gov. They do script breakdowns, photo files (36hr turnaround claimed), and state-property liaison. **Use them for the Green Ridge Youth Center conversation.**
- **No Allegany County film office.** The Mountain Maryland Film Festival (launched 2024, Allegany Arts Council) is a festival, not a commission. Chair Griffin Detrick (2026 committee); Executive Director Julie Westendorff. Arts Council: 109 Baltimore St, 301-777-2787, art@alleganyarts.org. Worth a courtesy call — they know every historic property steward in town.
- **Maryland Small Film Tax Credit:** 28% of qualified in-state spend, capped at $125,000 per project. Requires >$25k in-state spend and 50% of filming in MD. 10% of FY26's $20M authorization carved out for small/independent films. **Spruce Hollow with Cumberland-area supplementary shooting would qualify for the small-film carve-out if the producing entity is MD-domiciled ≥3 months.**
- **Cumberland city film permits:** Community Development Dept., 57 N Liberty St, 301-722-2000 x5600. No dedicated film ordinance; permits handled case-by-case.

### Crew Base — VERDICT: IMPORT FROM PITTSBURGH/DC/BALTIMORE
- No substantial local crew pool. ProductionHub lists for Cumberland are thin and dominated by corporate video shops. Beverly Boy Productions has a "Cumberland Film Crew" offering but it's an aggregator, not a local roster.
- **Nearest real crew hubs:**
  - **Pittsburgh** (2hr via US-219/I-76): strongest regional option. Union and non-union pool, full G&E rental, proximity to filming economies (Mindhunter, Rust Belt Studios, American Rust).
  - **Washington DC / Baltimore** (2.5hr via I-68/I-70): Red Star Rentals (DC + Baltimore), Afterglow Lighting, Hit & Run, Professional Film/Video Service.
  - **Morgantown WV** (1hr via I-68): small but growing — WVU film students, some indie-horror crew thanks to Trans-Allegheny proximity.
  - **Harrisburg/State College** (2.5–3hr): Lampcandy grip & electric, 717-461-2548.

### Frostburg State University
- **Film Studies minor only** (not a production major). Hosted Maceo Tendaj workshop April 2025.
- A minor program produces PAs and enthusiasts, not trained grips/gaffers. But **it's your local student-labor pool** — free interns, some equipment access, and the program's faculty can open doors to campus locations (Old Main, Compton, Performing Arts Center) that have the institutional-masonry look.
- **Action:** Contact the Department of Communication (which houses the Film Studies minor) via frostburg.edu/findit.php. Offer a paid PA/intern program in exchange for location access and student labor. This is the cheapest crew augmentation in the region.
- Frostburg Palace Theatre (1904 nickelodeon origin, still operating) is a potential screening/premiere venue for local press and community goodwill.

### Equipment Rental — CLOSEST
- **Pittsburgh:** multiple full-service houses
- **DC:** Red Star Pictures (camera/lens/grip/lighting) — easiest delivery path to Cumberland via I-270/I-70/I-68
- **Lampcandy (PA):** 717-461-2548, delivers into the DC/Baltimore corridor
- **Local minimal:** Hit & Run serves Hagerstown/Cumberland for post/small packages

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

**Use Cumberland as a supplementary/establishing unit anchored at Green Ridge, not as a primary production base.** Book 5–7 days of Cumberland-area shooting for:
- Exterior atmospherics (downtown, Masonic Temple exterior, Mt. Savage Castle exterior)
- Period interior inserts (Footer Building boiler/utility spaces, Allegany Museum courtroom, Masonic lodge)
- Railroad backstory (WMSR shops or station)
- Emmanuel Church tunnels (if access negotiated — this is the sleeper pick)
- Forest / travel sequences (Green Ridge State Forest — already planned)

Anchor the facility interiors/exteriors at **Trans-Allegheny** (53 mi SW) or **Pennhurst** (174 mi NE). Budget travel/housing for a DC or Pittsburgh crew.

**Single highest-leverage action right now:** Email Catherine Batavick at MFO with a logline and ask specifically about (a) Green Ridge Youth Center access timeline post-FY2027 closure, and (b) state small-film tax credit pre-qualification for a MindInMotion production. That one email opens every state-owned door in this region.
