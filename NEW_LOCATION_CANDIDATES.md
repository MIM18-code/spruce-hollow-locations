# Spruce Hollow — New Location Candidates

**Date:** April 16, 2026
**Compiled for:** Joshua Land
**Scope:** Net-new candidates to add to Tier 1 (Trans-Allegheny, Pennhurst). Every entry below was not on the prior eliminated/evaluated list.

---

## TL;DR

The Tier 1 bench is not as thin as it looks. There are at least three strong net-new candidates worth calling this week: **SCI Cresson (PA)**, **West Virginia Penitentiary at Moundsville (WV)**, and **Ohio State Reformatory (OH)**. A fourth — **Holmesburg Prison (PA)** — is live but harder to book without GPFO muscle. Everything else from the deep-research list is either demolished, redeveloped, under active fire damage, or blocked by an active facility (Central State VA, Norwich CT, Hudson River NY, Mayview/Dixmont PA, Richardson Olmsted NY is hotel-converted).

All four recommendations below sit inside the six-hour ring from Cumberland, MD. Moundsville (2h 34m) and Cresson (~2h) are inside the Trans-Allegheny travel zone. All four have documented feature-film credits. Three have established rentals staff and price sheets. Pair WV Moundsville with Trans-Allegheny and you sit on a 27% + 4% refundable WV credit with two verified institutional locations inside 90 miles of each other.

---

## Ranked Shortlist

### 1. SCI Cresson — Cresson, PA (Cambria County)
**Status:** Abandoned / privately owned / legal access infrastructure in place.
**Travel from Cumberland:** ~2 hours (US-219 N to US-22 W via Altoona). Well inside the WV/MD tax-credit commuting window.

**Why it's the top pick for Spruce Hollow:**
- Former tuberculosis sanatorium (built 1913) that became a state correctional institution; closed June 30, 2013. ([Uncovering PA](https://uncoveringpa.com/abandoned-sci-cresson-prison))
- ~20 intact buildings on 34 accessible acres including: the original sanatorium, **modern two-story cell blocks, older three-story cell blocks**, prison hospital, chapel, kitchen, yard, gymnasium. ([Abandoned Online](https://www.abandonedspaces.com/prisons/state-correctional-institution-cresson.html))
- Owner: **Big House Produce LLC** (hydroponic farm operator that bought the site in 2018 and runs it as a dual-use working farm + urbex/paranormal/photography destination). Ownership is documented and commercially motivated. ([WJAC](https://wjactv.com/news/local/local-tourism-company-purchases-former-cresson-sanatorium-will-offer-guided-tours))
- Current public-tour pricing: $40 for a 3-hour self-guided slot, $80 all-day. That's a floor, not a ceiling — multi-day buyouts are how they monetize the off-season. ([PA Bucket List](https://pabucketlist.com/exploring-the-abandoned-cresson-state-prison-in-cambria-county/))
- Site is already used as a police/military airsoft training facility, so they are experienced with liability, indemnification, and large crew access. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Correctional_Institution_%E2%80%93_Cresson))

**PA tax credit stacks:** 25% base, 30% with Qualified Production Facility (transferable, $100M annual cap). ([Film in PA](https://filminpa.com/incentives/))

**First-call contact:**
- Primary web contact: https://www.cressonsanatorium.com/tours — the site uses TicketLeap/HauntPay; primary booking channel is linktr.ee/bighouseproduce. I could not find a published phone number for Big House Produce. Route through the contact form and the Facebook page (@cressonsanatorium) — they respond fast on Facebook Messenger because tour logistics run through it.
- Backup: Film Pittsburgh / Pittsburgh Film Office — (412) 261-2744 — for permitting support in Cambria County.

**What to ask:**
1. Multi-week exclusive-use rate card for a 20–30 day feature shoot in Sept–Nov 2026 (their slow season).
2. Specifically: cell block access (both the three-story older units and the two-story modern units), prison hospital, kitchen, chapel.
3. Whether airsoft/paranormal/tour bookings can be paused for the shoot window (they can — this is how they made their money on *Ghost Adventures* type gigs).
4. Insurance minimums, power/HVAC, lock-up/art department storage.
5. Whether the hydroponic operation restricts any specific buildings.

**Dealbreaker watch:** None confirmed. The only risk is that the hydroponic farm occupies part of the complex, which may reduce the sellable footprint. Confirm on site scout.

### 2. West Virginia Penitentiary — Moundsville, WV
**Status:** Decommissioned 1995, owned by Moundsville Economic Development Council (MEDC), actively operating as museum + film location.
**Travel from Cumberland:** 164 miles, 2h 34m on I-68 W. ([Travelmath](https://www.travelmath.com/distance/from/Moundsville,+WV/to/Cumberland,+MD))

**Why it ranks high:**
- Full feature credits: *Silence of the Lambs* (1991 — Hannibal Lecter's cell scenes), *Fool's Parade* (1971), *Out of the Furnace* (2013), Hulu's *Castle Rock* (2017) where it stood in for Shawshank State Prison. Plus *Ghost Adventures*, *Ghost Hunters*. ([The Intelligencer](https://www.theintelligencer.net/news/top-headlines/2017/09/moundsville-meets-maine-as-filming-wraps-at-former-west-virginia-penitentiary/), [Weelunk](https://weelunk.com/moundsville-at-the-movies-penitentiary-proves-a-haunting-draw-for-hollywood/))
- Self-describes as "tourist attraction, museum, training facility, and filming location." ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Virginia_Penitentiary))
- **WV tax credit stacks here (27% + 4% = 31% refundable) — same jurisdiction as Trans-Allegheny.** Putting both in the same production puts you in one incentive pool.
- Gothic sandstone main building, four cell blocks (Old Man Colony, North Hall, South Hall, New Wall), original execution chamber, sugar shack (inmate bar), chapel. Interior cell/corridor spaces intact.

**First-call contact:**
- **WV Penitentiary (WVPenTours): (304) 845-6200** ([Yelp](https://www.yelp.com/biz/west-virginia-penitentiary-tours-moundsville))
- Secondary: **(304) 845-9810 ext. 403**
- Address: 818 Jefferson Ave, Moundsville, WV 26041
- Yelp shows "temporarily closed" listing as of Jan 2026 — verify operating status on first call. This may be a seasonal closure (they're typically open Tue–Sun, Mar–Nov).

**What to ask:**
1. Film rental rate for a 20–30 day buyout and whether that blocks public tours or whether they want to continue tours around the shoot (they did this for *Castle Rock*).
2. WV Film Office coordination — ask them to loop in Pam Haynes Martin (WV Film Office, 304-957-9382) for tax credit certification; they'll know the process.
3. Confirm active use as a Corrections Academy training site (DCR runs training weeks here) — those weeks will be blackout dates for filming.
4. Power infrastructure, generator pad access, crew parking on the yard.

**Dealbreaker watch:** The facility shares the block with the active Northern Correctional Facility and DCR training academy. Training days are hard blackouts. But MEDC runs the pen separately from DCR and has blocked-off weeks for feature shoots before — this is a scheduling issue, not a lockout.

### 3. Ohio State Reformatory — Mansfield, OH
**Status:** Operated by **Mansfield Reformatory Preservation Society (MRPS)**, an established 501(c)(3) with a dedicated events/rentals staff.
**Travel from Cumberland:** 282 miles, ~4h 26m via I-76 / PA Turnpike West. ([Travelmath](https://www.travelmath.com/distance/))

**Why it's on the list:**
- This is the *Shawshank Redemption* facility. Plus *Air Force One* (1997), *Escape Plan: The Extractors* (2019), *Judas and the Black Messiah* (2021 — solitary + West Cell Block scenes). ([Destination Mansfield](https://destinationmansfield.com/film-mansfield/))
- Verified **dedicated rentals liaison** (Ashleigh Ramey) and verified **county film liaison** (Jodie Snavely at Destination Mansfield).
- Six-story East Cell Block (the world's largest freestanding steel cell block), 250,000 sq ft total interior. Warden's quarters, solitary wing, chapel, hospital wing, administration.
- **Ohio tax credit: 30% refundable on all cast, crew, and production spend** — refundable, not transferable. That's stronger than PA's transferable model. ([Ohio Dev Services](https://development.ohio.gov/))

**First-call contact:**
- **Ashleigh Ramey, MRPS Rentals: (419) 522-2644** — ashleigh@mrps.org
- County backup: **Jodie Snavely, Destination Mansfield Special Projects: (419) 525-1300** — jsnavely@destinationmansfield.com
- Ohio Film Office (tax credit): **(614) 644-5156** — askohiofilm@development.ohio.gov
- Address: 100 Reformatory Rd, Mansfield, OH 44905

**What to ask:**
1. 20–30 day exclusive buyout rate and whether that includes day-tour blackouts.
2. Whether they have a feature-film rate card vs. photo/commercial rate card (they do).
3. Ohio Motion Picture Tax Credit application timing — state certifies in advance.
4. Standing insurance requirement (they work with features regularly — they'll have a template).

**Dealbreaker watch:** Distance. 4.5 hours from Cumberland is at the edge of the acceptable window. If the director wants talent based in Pittsburgh/DC, this is workable; if the anchor crew is coming from WV, Moundsville and Cresson are both closer and cheaper on per diem. Also: Mansfield is a well-oiled tourism operation, which means higher rates than Cresson but faster contracting.

### 4. Holmesburg Prison — Philadelphia, PA
**Status:** Owned by **City of Philadelphia Prison System**, mostly vacant since 1995, occasionally used for overflow and work programs. Film access is brokered by the Greater Philadelphia Film Office (GPFO).
**Travel from Cumberland:** ~4h 30m via I-70/I-76.

**Why it's on the list:**
- Verified feature credits: *Law Abiding Citizen* (2009), *Animal Factory* (2000), *Up Close & Personal* (1996), *Condition Red* (1995), *Against the Night* (2017 — shot entirely on location). ([WHYY](https://whyy.org/articles/7-movies-shot-inside-philly-prisons/))
- Radial wagon-wheel plan with intact cell blocks, yard, administration. Visually distinct from Eastern State (less romantic, more brutal) — arguably *more* appropriate for a juvenile detention setting than Eastern State's picturesque Gothic.
- Contemporary operation means power and security are live — crew doesn't have to bring everything in.

**First-call contact:**
- **Sharon Pinkenson, Executive Director, Greater Philadelphia Film Office: (215) 686-2668** — pinkenson@film.org
- Address: One Parkway Building, 1515 Arch Street, 11th Floor, Philadelphia, PA 19102
- GPFO is the access broker. They deal with the city prison system on behalf of productions.

**What to ask:**
1. Current availability — confirm it's not being used for overflow in the shoot window.
2. Whether GPFO handles permit bundling with the city directly.
3. Use against PA 25%/30% tax credit (it qualifies; GPFO will process).
4. Whether they can walk you through Holmesburg AND Eastern State on the same scout day (they can — both are GPFO-administered).

**Dealbreaker watch:**
- **Active use for work programs / overflow.** Dates are not guaranteed in advance. If Philly needs the building for intake surge, your shoot moves.
- **Reputational baggage.** Holmesburg has documented dermatological/biochemical inmate experimentation history (Kligman trials, 1951–1974) and a 1968 report documenting hundreds of inmate rapes. This is not a dealbreaker for a horror film — arguably it's thematically aligned — but any press kit needs to handle the site's history carefully. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holmesburg_Prison))
- Distance from Cumberland is similar to Mansfield and farther from the WV tax-credit zone.

## Locations I Checked and Eliminated

- **Western State Hospital / DeJarnette (Staunton, VA):** Partially redeveloped as Blackburn Inn. DeJarnette building is controlled by Frontier Culture Museum which is actively working to demolish or redevelop the site for commercial use. Anti-trespassing infrastructure installed Feb 2025. ([WHSV](https://www.whsv.com/2025/02/08/anti-trespassing-measures-taken-abandoned-dejarnette-sanitorium/)) Not film-available.
- **Central State Hospital (Petersburg, VA):** Active DBHDS psychiatric facility; the historic Kirkbride is demolished. Eliminated.
- **Hudson River State Hospital (NY):** Actively being redeveloped as $300M "Hudson Heritage" mixed-use. Multiple fires (2007, 2018). Access restricted. ([Abandoned America](https://www.abandonedamerica.us/hudson-river-state-hospital))
- **Norwich State Hospital (CT):** ~All buildings demolished by 2019. Recent fires July 2025 and Oct 2025. Not viable.
- **The Ridges / Athens Lunatic Asylum (OH):** Owned by Ohio University. Public access restricted to Kennedy Museum only; no tours. Access requires special permission with no public track record of feature approvals. Possible but high-friction.
- **Richardson Olmsted (Buffalo, NY):** Converted to The Richardson Hotel + event lawn; abandoned wards only viewable on seasonal docent tours. No established feature-film program. 7+ hour drive from Cumberland — outside the window.
- **Kentucky State Reformatory (LaGrange, KY):** Inmates were being transitioned out as of 2023 but the facility remains operational. Not abandoned. 8+ hours from Cumberland regardless.
- **Dixmont / Mayview (PA):** Both demolished.
- **Embreeville State Hospital (PA):** Private ownership (2012 sale) with proposed 1,100-home development. No film access track record.
- **Scotland School for Veterans' Children (PA):** Owned by Winebrenner Theological Seminary; deteriorating but access is unclear and no feature credits.
- **Lorton Reformatory (VA):** Fully converted to Workhouse Arts Center. Not abandoned aesthetic anymore.

## Eastern State Penitentiary — Worth a Note

Not new — you likely already know it — but verified current contact: **Francine Schiffman, Assoc. Director of Group Sales & Events, (215) 236-5111 x240**. Their public language says rentals are "April through August" and "case-by-case" — which is hedge language for "we don't want to disrupt the Halloween haunt revenue stream." They have done features (*Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen*, *12 Monkeys*), but the haunt season (mid-Sept through early Nov) is a hard no. If your shoot lands in the summer, it's a call worth making. If you're shooting October, don't bother. ([Eastern State](https://www.easternstate.org/visit/site-rentals/photography-filmmaking))

## Summary: Is the Tier 1 Bench Thin?

No. The bench is deeper than the prior search indicated, but you have to widen the frame beyond "Maryland abandoned juvenile facility." The correct frame is "intact institutional decay within 6 hours of Cumberland with an owner who wants your production money." Using that frame:

- **Three production-ready sites with feature-film infrastructure already built in:** SCI Cresson, WV Pen Moundsville, Ohio State Reformatory.
- **Two more that require brokering:** Holmesburg (via GPFO), Eastern State (summer only).
- **Your Tier 1 pair (Trans-Allegheny + Pennhurst) is actually Tier 1 of 5, not Tier 1 of 2.**

**Strongest recommendation:** Add **WV Pen Moundsville** to the Tier 1 bench alongside Trans-Allegheny. Same tax credit jurisdiction, 90 minutes apart, both verified feature-friendly, both have handled multi-week shoots. That pairing alone gives you enough institutional visual range to cover 20–30 days without anyone noticing you split the location.

**Second recommendation:** Call SCI Cresson this week. If the ownership is as commercially motivated as the evidence suggests, Cresson is the sleeper pick — closest to Cumberland, intact modern cell blocks (not Gothic asylum, actual *prison* geometry), and the least-filmed of the four. That means fewer audience associations with *Shawshank* / *Silence of the Lambs* pulling the audience out of Spruce Hollow.
