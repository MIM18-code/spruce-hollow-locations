# SPRUCE HOLLOW — PRIVATELY OWNED FORMER INSTITUTIONAL BUILDINGS

**Date:** April 16, 2026
**Scope:** Long-shot / high-upside category. Privately owned former institutional buildings (orphanages, reform schools, military academies, Catholic retreats, private sanatoria) that are **not** operating as commercial tour businesses. Owners are family trusts, estates, LLCs, foundations, or individuals — the target is the motivated holder who'll take $1–3K/week or a credit for a vacant shell.
**For:** Joshua Land / MindInMotion
**Budget lens:** $237K–290K total. Rental target $1–3K/week, credit-for-access acceptable.

---

## TL;DR — READ THIS FIRST

The public-record research wall is real. County tax-sale databases and assessor portals do not expose property-use codes in a queryable way for most of the target region (Allegany MD, Cambria PA, Lewis WV), and LLC-held institutional shells don't surface in normal real-estate search. The 12 months of web-surfaceable auction catalogs, preservation-easement registries, and local news sweeps yielded **three genuine candidates** worth a call this week. Everything else in this category either (a) got demolished, (b) got bought by a university or religious organization that will be present on-site, or (c) is an active business dressed up as an abandoned building (most "abandoned" Odd Fellows properties).

The three real candidates, in order of fit:

1. **Scotland Campus — Scotland, PA** — former Scotland School for Veterans' Children. 185 acres, 75 buildings, 1892 tunnel system, owner in Chapter 11 as of January 2026. 1h 42min from Cumberland. Already listed on LocationsHub as a film-available property. **This is the top lead in this entire category.**

2. **Historic Pence Springs Resort / former Greenbrier Academy — Pence Springs, WV** — 138 acres, 44,000 SF of interior space, intact women's-prison cell block on the 3rd floor, actively listed for sale at $2.2M. ~3h 24min from Cumberland. Same WV tax-credit jurisdiction as Trans-Allegheny + Moundsville. Motivated seller in a down-market listing.

3. **Carson Long Military Academy — New Bloomfield, PA** — 1836 former military academy, sold to Talmudic University in 2020 for $730K, used as a summer yeshiva program only. Vacant ~9 months of the year. 1-800 boys' boarding-school architecture. ~3h from Cumberland.

Plus one **future lead**: **Valley Forge Military Academy — Wayne, PA** is closing May 9, 2026. Eastern University is taking 33 acres; Radnor Township is moving on 14 acres by eminent domain; the remaining ~23 acres and buildings are in limbo. For a fall 2026 shoot, this is a timing play — the main academic buildings will be vacant with no clear near-term buyer. Too far from Cumberland (4h+) to anchor the shoot, but worth a scout call as a one-off block.

**What this research category does not deliver:** There is no hidden family-estate orphanage with a cooperative widow in the Western Maryland / Eastern West Virginia corridor. The Odd Fellows / Masonic / Shriners pipeline leads to Missouri (Liberty, Belvoir Winery) or to active care facilities (Elizabethtown Masonic Children's Home, Mars MHY, Hoffman Homes). The one real PA Odd Fellows orphanage (Sunbury) auctioned in 2023 to an unidentified buyer. Follow-up below.

## CANDIDATE 1 — SCOTLAND CAMPUS (Scotland, PA)

**Status:** Private LLC owner in Chapter 11 bankruptcy as of January 1, 2026. 185-acre former state school. Registered on LocationsHub as a filming location.

**Property:**
- 3583 Scotland Rd, Scotland, PA 17254 (Franklin County, zip is near Chambersburg)
- 185 acres, 75 buildings per 2026 bankruptcy filings (Locations Hub says 69 buildings; 2023 sale docs say 182-185 acres). Includes farm house, barn, cottages, gymnasiums, football field, amphitheater, creek, pond, railroad tracks, and an **underground tunnel system dating to c.1892-1893**
- Original use: Scotland School for Veterans' Children, established 1895 by PA GAR veterans, closed 2009 — serves as a literal former state-run residential youth campus, which is what Spruce Hollow depicts
- Condition: Actively being "renovated" by summer-camp operator; most buildings are vacant off-season. Third-party listings describe mix of restored common areas and many untouched original dorm/classroom buildings

**Ownership trail:**
- 2009: PA closes the school
- 2013: Sold to Winebrenner Theological Seminary (per Herald-Mail)
- 2018-2022: Bounced between operators under the "Scotland Campus" brand (prep school, sports academy, charter applications, migrant-housing proposal)
- 2022: Gable Holdings CM-1 LLC takes ownership via foreclosure/purchase
- 2023: Sold to **MSLH LLC** for $9M. MSLH is a PA-registered LLC with mailing address in Lakewood, NJ
- 2026 (Jan 1): MSLH LLC files Chapter 11 in EDNY bankruptcy court, case 26-40002, triggered by a $674K mechanics-lien judgment and a scheduled Jan 9 sheriff's sale by creditor For-Med Supply and Repair LLC. Reported assets $17M, liabilities $13.5M, $6.2M mortgage to Gable Holdings CM-1 LLC
- **Owners of record:** Chaya Bajnon and Abraham Bajnon (50/50). Abraham signed the bankruptcy petition as managing member
- Current use: Seasonal Hasidic/Orthodox Jewish summer camp, June–August; girls in July, boys in August. Off-season the campus is essentially vacant.

**Access path:**
1. **Primary:** LocationsHub listing — id 043-10070694. LocationsHub is a marketplace for film locations; production inquiries go through the platform. This is the path of least resistance because the owner already put the building on a film-location platform.
2. **Secondary (direct):** Scotland Campus, scotlandcampus.org. Graham Messner is listed on LinkedIn as Chief Development Officer — the non-bankruptcy public face. Worth a direct email before routing through counsel.
3. **Tertiary (leverage the bankruptcy):** The Chapter 11 debtor is motivated to generate revenue to show the court. Counsel of record is **Charles Wertman, Law Offices of Charles Wertman P.C.** In a Chapter 11, the debtor-in-possession needs court approval for out-of-ordinary-course transactions, but a location rental is ordinary-course for a campus that is already listed on LocationsHub. A $1–3K/week location fee goes into the DIP account and makes everyone happier. Mention the bankruptcy explicitly when you call — it de-risks the conversation because the creditor motivation is clear.

**Filming history:** Actively listed on LocationsHub (a real industry platform, not Peerspace). No verified feature credits surfaced in web search. Ask for their LocationsHub inquiry log on the first call — the operator likely has photo-shoot or small-production history from the platform.

**Distance from Cumberland:** **87 miles, 1h 42min** via US-40. Well inside the one-way-drive window. **This is the closest legitimate long-shot candidate in this category.**

**PA tax credit:** 25% base, 30% with Qualified Production Facility designation. Filminpa.com, transferable credit, $100M annual cap.

**Dealbreaker watch:**
- Summer months (June–August) are locked for the Hasidic camp. Shoot Sept–May only.
- Bankruptcy means decisions take longer and may require court approval; build 2-3 extra weeks into contracting.
- Many buildings are genuinely mid-renovation; don't assume all 75 are shootable. Ask for building-by-building condition on scout.
- 500 summer campers on-site means the owner has a real liability framework; they've already signed off on large-group insurance minimums. This cuts both ways: they know what to ask for.

**First call script:** "Hi, we're an indie feature looking at your LocationsHub listing for a fall 2026 shoot. We understand the property is in Chapter 11 — we'd like to talk about a 20-30 day exclusive buyout at a rate that works for the court. What's the best path to get that conversation started?"

**Status:** For sale, $2.2M. 138.51 acres. Vacant since Greenbrier Academy for Girls (therapeutic boarding school) closed in 2023. Recently renovated common areas; 3rd-floor prison-era cells intact.

**Property:**

## HOW THIS CATEGORY'S RESEARCH WALLS WORK

Joshua — direct read: this category does not expose cleanly through public records. Here's what the methods actually returned and what they didn't:

### What worked
1. **Bankruptcy filings** — Chapter 11 dockets are the single best source for distressed institutional property ownership because the debtor must disclose assets, secured creditors, and management. MSLH LLC / Scotland Campus was only traceable because the owner filed Chapter 11 three months ago. Bondoro.com indexes recent Chapter 11 filings; worth a standing query for "Pennsylvania + real estate + nonprofit" every 30 days until production.
2. **Real-estate listings for institutional properties** — Foxfire Realty, Land.com, Crexi, LandWatch, LandHub will surface institutional former-use properties if you search by acreage (>100 acres) + "former school/hotel/resort" + state. The Pence Springs listing was found this way.
3. **LocationsHub** — the actual industry film-location database. Both Scotland Campus and Pence Springs will have listings on LocationsHub or similar if the owner is film-aware. LocationsHub requires a production account to see full contact details.
4. **Local news obits for institutions** — a Chambersburg Franklin County newspaper article about migrant-housing proposals surfaced Scotland Campus because local reporters cover failed-proposal cycles. Similar pattern for Sunbury Daily Item on the Odd Fellows orphanage auction.

### What did not work (and why)
1. **County assessor/tax-sale databases (Allegany MD, Cambria PA, Lewis WV)** do not expose property-use codes queryably. You get a parcel map, an owner name, and an assessed value, but no "former school" flag. Searching them requires knowing the parcel ID or street address first — which is a chicken-and-egg problem.
2. **National Register of Historic Places** is searchable but the "institution" category isn't a formal NRHP classification — you'd have to hand-review thousands of listings.
3. **State preservation easement registries** (MHT Maryland, PA Historic Preservation Trust) list easement-encumbered properties but don't distinguish "institutional character." And easement restrictions frequently limit filming alterations.
4. **HUD surplus-property inventory** is federal surplus — almost none of it is institutional shell property in the right geography at the right price.
5. **LLC filings for institutional addresses** would surface Scotland Campus's MSLH LLC, but only if you already knew the address. PA Department of State and WV Secretary of State LLC searches are keyword-weak.

### What to do about the wall — practical recommendations

If this category matters to the film, three actions get around the public-records dead-end:

1. **Hire a Pennsylvania-based location scout with relationships to regional commercial realtors.** Foxfire Realty (David Sibray) in southern WV, Beiler-Campbell in Chester County PA, and NAI Global affiliates in Central PA all have "we've been trying to sell this school for 3 years" inventory that never hits a web listing. A $1K-2K scout day-rate with one of these realtors' local contacts will surface 3-5 real candidates I can't see from a national web search.

2. **Post a location-wanted listing on LocationsHub, Giggster, and Reel Scout.** Film-aware owners who don't know they have a sellable asset will respond. Write the listing specifically: "intact institutional corridor, cell or dorm block preferred, Sept-Dec 2026, PA/MD/WV, $1-3K/week, will credit on-screen." This reverses the search problem.

3. **Northumberland County, PA direct records request.** The 2023 Odd Fellows Orphan Home auction at 1810 Snydertown Rd, Sunbury was the most promising unresolved lead. Whoever bought that 40-acre campus in May 2023 is a film-ready private owner. Northumberland County Recorder of Deeds will have the post-May-2023 deed transfer on file. A $5 records request or a phone call (570-988-4151) closes the loop faster than web search ever will.
