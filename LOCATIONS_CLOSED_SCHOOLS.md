# SPRUCE HOLLOW — CLOSED SCHOOLS AS JUVENILE DETENTION STAND-INS

**Prepared:** April 16, 2026
**Author:** Location research pass, Appalachian/Mid-Atlantic 4-hour radius from Cumberland, MD
**Purpose:** K-12 school buildings closed 2005–present that can double as abandoned juvenile detention facility at $500–2,000/day. Mid-century brick institutional architecture reads as "reform school" far better than any of the commercial prison rental properties (TALA, Pennhurst, OSR, Cresson) at 1/20th the price.

## QUICK WINS — CALL THESE FIRST

### 1. MAPLETON-UNION ELEMENTARY — Mapleton Depot, PA (Huntingdon County)
**Status:** CLOSED 2020. CURRENTLY UP FOR SEALED BIDS (Jan 2026 solicitation). 7.5 acres. Vacant. Owned outright by Mount Union Area School District. Building and land, inspection by appointment. This is the single most actionable lead in the region — the district is literally trying to unload it *right now*.
**Why it's gold:** You can tour it for free tomorrow. If no buyer takes it, a film rental is exactly the kind of interim income-producing use the district will entertain. Even if it sells, the buyer will likely be a developer looking to recoup — rental to a film is bridge revenue.
**CALL:** Mount Union Area School District Superintendent's office — **(814) 542-8631**. Ask to inspect the former Mapleton-Union Elementary School. Mon–Fri 7am–3pm. Mailing: 603 North Industrial Drive, Mount Union, PA 17066.
**Distance from Cumberland:** ~100 miles (2 hr via US-30 E + I-99 N).

### 2. KITZMILLER ELEMENTARY — Kitzmiller, MD (Garrett County)
**Status:** CLOSED 2013. Building owned by Town of Kitzmiller. Partial occupancy (Head Start downstairs, after-school program, Helping Hands Thrift Shop) — but the upstairs classrooms, gymnasium, and corridor space are unused community hall. Mayor/Town Clerk controls access.
**Why it matters:** This is 50 minutes from Cumberland, in a tiny town (pop. ~300) that has actively invited proposals for the building's future use. Town of Kitzmiller literally surveyed residents in January 2025 about what to do with the building. They want income and activity. A film production is an unambiguous win for them.
**CALL:** Town of Kitzmiller Town Hall — **(301) 453-3449**. Email: kitzmd@shentel.net. Address: 104 West Centre St., Kitzmiller, MD 21538. (School itself at 288 West Main St.)
**Distance from Cumberland:** 48 miles (1 hr 15 min via US-220 S + MD-135).

### 3. PENN CAMBRIA PRIMARY — Lilly, PA (Cambria County)
**Status:** CLOSED end of 2024-25 school year. SOLD AUG 2025 to Graystone Inc. for $250,000. Private owner. Building is at 400 Main Street, Lilly, PA. Sits on ~1.5 acres across three parcels. Graystone is working with Lilly Borough on "community recreation" — they have NO confirmed commercial use yet, which means they'll rent the thing to someone paying. 1950s-era institutional primary school.
**Why it's gold:** Private LLC owner, no bureaucracy, hungry for revenue while they figure out their plans. Unoccupied.
**CALL:** Look up Graystone Inc. via Lilly Borough — Lilly Borough municipal office is a good starting point. Penn Cambria SD superintendent's office can also supply Graystone's contact: **(814) 886-8121** (PCSD main, 201 6th St, Cresson PA).
**Distance from Cumberland:** ~80 miles (1 hr 45 min via I-99 N).

## BUDGET IMPLICATIONS

Commercial institutional rentals (TALA, Pennhurst, OSR, Cresson) run **$15K–$40K/week** per the project brief. The closed-school alternatives identified here should land in the **$500–$3,000/day** range based on comparable rural-PA/WV public-body property rentals:
- Town of Kitzmiller: expect $500-1,000/day — they have no standard rate and will negotiate. A $10K total lease for a 2-week window is plausible.
- School districts (Hampshire, Preston, Mt Union): expect $1,000-2,500/day plus district's cost of custodial staff and utilities. A 3-week lease likely $25K-40K all-in.
- Private LLC (Graystone / Penn Cambria): negotiation-dependent. $1,500-3,000/day likely; could be lower if they need bridge revenue.

**For a 4-week principal shoot at any of these, you're looking at $15K-60K in location costs vs. $60K-160K for TALA-tier rental.** Savings of $45K-100K funds a lot of art department and crew housing.

## ARCHITECTURE NOTE

The script calls for mid-20th-century brick-institutional geometry — long double-loaded corridors, tile wainscot, industrial radiators, gymnasium with stage/curtain, auditorium with fixed seating, cafeteria with pass-through windows, boiler room, locker rooms. This is the **default construction vocabulary of rural Appalachian K-8 schools 1930-1965**. Mapleton-Union, Kitzmiller, Penn Cambria Primary, Fellowsville, and the Hampshire County trio all match this era. Rowlesburg (1987) does NOT — skip unless specifically for newer-building flashback beats.

The closed-school swap is structurally a better match for the script than the commercial asylums/prisons, which read as "prison" (adult correctional) rather than "juvenile reform school." Juvenile detention in the 1950s-1970s was explicitly modeled on school architecture with security overlays — a closed Appalachian elementary school in mid-century brick is closer to the real historical referent than any of the $25K/week paid rentals.
