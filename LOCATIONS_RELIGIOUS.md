# SPRUCE HOLLOW — Religious Institutions as Filming Locations

**Research scope:** Catholic / Episcopal / Orthodox religious properties within ~6 hours of Cumberland, MD that could substitute for an abandoned juvenile detention facility at $1K–5K/day instead of $15K–40K/week.

**Research date:** April 16, 2026

**TL;DR:** The category is mostly thinner than it looks on paper. Declining orders + aging novitiates produces a lot of press releases but very few buildings that are (a) still standing, (b) shootable (not gutted or converted to apartments), (c) accessible to cold-call productions. After working through 25+ candidates, there are **5 real leads worth pursuing**, 2 of which are unusually strong. One (the former Wernersville Jesuit Center) is very possibly the single best religious-institution candidate in the entire mid-Atlantic for this project right now — a 254-acre campus with a 1929 novitiate building that was owned by the Jesuits until March 2023, now owned by a senior-living nonprofit (Phoebe Ministries) that has no religious-order sensitivity to navigate. It's a ~3.5-hour drive from Cumberland.

### 1. Former Jesuit Center for Spiritual Growth (Wernersville, PA)
**Address:** ~501 N Church Road, Wernersville, PA 19565 (adjacent to the Phoebe Berks campus)
**Current owner:** Phoebe Ministries (acquired March 2023, announced publicly May 2024)
**Status:** Transitioning to senior living. The 1929 Novitiate Building is intact, 254 acres, adjacent to active Phoebe Berks senior living campus.
**Drive from Cumberland, MD:** ~3h 15m

**Why this is the best lead in the category:**
- English Renaissance institutional building, completed 1929, built for the Jesuits by philanthropists Nicholas and Genevieve Brady.
- Originally a novitiate: the building was designed around dormitory wings, long corridors, refectory, and a formal chapel — exactly the spatial grammar of an institutional juvenile facility.
- The Jesuits closed it in August 2021 because upkeep was $500K/year + needed major repairs. The building is old and in need of work — production-friendly condition (worn surfaces, original woodwork, age patina). Features carved oak, marble, coffered ceilings.
- **Already listed on LocationsHub** — a Reelscout-operated film location marketplace (URL: rs.locationshub.com/location_detail.aspx?id=043-10084203). This alone is nearly unheard of for a religious property — it signals that either the Jesuits or Phoebe have actively marketed it as a film location.
- Owner is a nonprofit senior-living operator, not a religious order. No bishop to navigate, no art loss concerns (the valuable religious art was already distributed to other Jesuit sites per Montco Today, Sep 2024).

**Contact path:**
- **Primary:** Phoebe Ministries media contacts page: phoebe.org/media-contacts/ (Alan Raisman, Public Relations). Main line: 610-435-9037.
- **Secondary:** LocationsHub listing directly (043-10084203). The listing's existence means someone responsible for the property has already agreed to inquiries.
- **Approach:** Pitch as a senior-living-adjacent nonprofit fundraiser — "we're a small horror indie, willing to make a contribution to Phoebe's mission in exchange for access to the Novitiate Building for 3 weeks." Phoebe is a health/care charity and has no religious baggage — frame the project as a respectful period piece set in an institutional building, not a horror shoot set in a religious space.

**Condition estimate:** Intact, aging, awaiting a conversion plan Phoebe hasn't fully announced. This window (2026) is the ideal moment — after the Jesuits left but before construction fully begins.

**Architecture present:** Dormitory wings ✅, long institutional corridors ✅, formal chapel ✅, refectory ✅, grand stair halls, industrial basements. Interior stonework, coffered ceilings, darkened oak paneling.

**Prior film history:** Unknown specific titles. Listed on LocationsHub but specific productions not in the public record.

**Sources:** [Phoebe Acquires the Jesuit Center (Phoebe Messenger Spring 2024)](https://phoebe.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/PhoebeMessenger_Spring24_Web.pdf) · [Jesuit Center closure announcement](https://www.jesuitseast.org/press-release/a-fond-farewell-to-wernersville-memories-of-seeking-gods-call/) · [LocationsHub listing](https://rs.locationshub.com/location_detail.aspx?id=043-10084203) · [WFMZ coverage](https://www.wfmz.com/news/area/berks/jesuit-center-in-berks-winds-down-after-50-years/article_9ea32e9c-f4a7-11eb-927f-1f2c1c3a51c2.html) · [Architectural description](https://www.paulandersonphotography.com/the-jesuit-center-wernersville-pa)

### 2. St. Francis Retreat House / Former Franciscan Friary (Easton, PA)
**Address:** 3918 Chipman Road, Easton, PA 18045
**Current owner:** Monsignor Robert J. Coll Foundation, Inc.
**Status:** Closed October 6, 2024 after 63 years. Planned reopening Fall 2026 as the "St. Francis Spirituality and Retreat Center of Easton."
**Drive from Cumberland, MD:** ~4h

**Why this is a strong lead:**
- 22 acres, 1961-built institutional friary — institutional-scale dormitory corridors, chapel, refectory, meeting halls.
- Current vacancy window runs from Oct 2024 through reopening in Fall 2026 — this is exactly when a shoot could happen with minimal conflict.
- Owner is a lay Catholic foundation (the Coll Foundation), not the Franciscans directly. Lay Catholic foundations are typically more business-minded about filming rentals than orders are.
- Bloom for Women (a nonprofit serving trafficking survivors) has already been allowed to occupy offices on the property, indicating the Foundation has an adaptive-use mindset.
- Franciscan architecture is plainer than Jesuit — wooden crosses, simple corridors, open chapel — but that often reads better as institutional (not churchy) on camera.

**Contact path:**
- Monsignor Robert J. Coll Foundation, Inc. — operating address 3918 Chipman Road, Easton, PA. Find via the Diocese of Allentown (allentowndiocese.org) — the Foundation is a named beneficiary of Msgr. Coll's estate and the Diocese is the likely routing contact.
- Diocese of Allentown general: 610-437-0755.
- Secondary: Friars of the Province of Our Lady of Guadalupe (former stewards): friars.us.

**Condition estimate:** Recently closed, fully furnished, in working order. Excellent shootable condition — was operating retreats until late 2024.

**Architecture present:** Dormitory-style retreat rooms ✅, institutional corridors ✅, chapel ✅, dining hall ✅, meeting rooms, 22 acres of grounds.

**Prior film history:** None documented.

**Sources:** [Diocese of Allentown closure announcement](https://www.allentowndiocese.org/news/st-francis-retreat-house-closes-doors-last-time) · [Friars.us Mass of Thanksgiving](https://friars.us/article/2024/11/01/mass-of-thanksgiving-celebrated-at-st.-francis-retreat-house) · [Valley Ledger on Bloom for Women move-in](https://www.thevalleyledger.com/?p=148234) · [WFMZ coverage](https://www.wfmz.com/news/area/lehighvalley/easton-catholic-retreat-site-to-close-due-to-low-attendance/article_c886af00-5b19-11ef-8da5-e3804c734e6c.html)

### 3. Wheeling University (formerly Wheeling Jesuit) — Campus owned by Diocese
**Address:** 316 Washington Avenue, Wheeling, WV 26003
**Current owner:** Roman Catholic Diocese of Wheeling-Charleston (bought the campus in 2017 for ~$32M in bond debt payoff). University leases it back.
**Status:** University still operates, but heavily downsized since 2019 — theology, philosophy, history, literature, and engineering programs eliminated; 20 faculty cut. Significantly more empty space than enrolled students.
**Drive from Cumberland, MD:** ~2h 30m

**Why this is a live lead:**
- The 65-acre, 15-building campus has residence halls (Sara Tracy, Campion, Ignatius) built as Jesuit-era dorms with institutional corridors.
- Whelan Hall (1955) was the Jesuit community residence — same novitiate architecture as Wernersville.
- The Diocese of Wheeling-Charleston has been in a long financial squeeze and sold non-church assets aggressively. They own the land. A production fee goes directly to the Diocese.
- Because the university is academically gutted, they have empty residence hall capacity, and summer filming around academic calendar is very feasible.

**Contact path:**
- Diocese of Wheeling-Charleston — main: 304-233-0880. Office of Communications is the first call.
- Wheeling University directly — main: 304-243-2000. Facilities office handles location inquiries.
- West Virginia Film Office (wvtourism.com/film) is the third route for intermediary support.

**Condition estimate:** Active university, maintained. Not distressed; may require art-department dressing to read as abandoned.

**Architecture present:** Residence hall corridors ✅, dormitory rooms ✅, chapel (Donahue Chapel) ✅, dining hall ✅, classrooms, library, gym.

**Prior film history:** None documented.

**Sources:** [Wheeling Jesuit Sells Campus to Diocese (Inside Higher Ed)](https://www.insidehighered.com/quicktakes/2017/05/24/wheeling-jesuit-sells-campus-diocese) · [2019 restructuring coverage](https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2019/04/05/two-years-after-rescue-wheeling-jesuit-guts-faculty-programs) · [Wheeling University residence halls](https://wheeling.edu/student-life/living-on-campus/residence-halls/)

## STRATEGIC NOTES

**Why religious is harder than it looks:** The core problem is that "closed due to declining orders" usually leads to one of three outcomes, and two of them kill the location:
1. **Sold to developer → demolished or gutted.** (Gilmary, Mount de Chantal, St. Mary's Ilchester.)
2. **Sold to religious-adjacent nonprofit → still in use.** (Mount St. Agnes to JHU, St. Charles Borromeo to Main Line Health.)
3. **Sold to secular operator with a transition window before reuse.** ← This is the only sweet spot, and it's where Wernersville and Easton sit right now.

**Why Wernersville is unusually good:** It's in the window. Phoebe bought it March 2023, hasn't broken ground on senior-living conversion, the Jesuits have fully vacated, and the building is listed on LocationsHub. A shoot in 2026 is the last clean window before whatever Phoebe builds.

**Approach language for outreach:** Religious owners respond poorly to "horror film." Call it a "suspense drama about institutional memory," "a period drama set in a 1970s state facility," or similar. Emphasize: (a) no exterior crosses/religious iconography on camera, (b) no desecration of chapel space, (c) a donation or fee that directly supports the current mission (Phoebe's senior care, Coll Foundation's retreat reopening). Offer a vetting of the script for anything offensive.

**One non-religious alternative that keeps surfacing:** SCI Cresson / Cresson State Prison / Cresson Sanatorium — privately owned since 2013, legally open for self-guided tours, one of the largest abandoned institutional properties in Pennsylvania, 1913 Tudor Revival. Sits 20 minutes from Mount Aloysius. Not religious, but it's the single closest shootable-abandoned-institutional-architecture match to your script within 2 hours of Cumberland. If no religious option lands at $1-5K/day, this is where I'd pivot.

**Source:** [Cresson Sanatorium](https://www.cressonsanatorium.com/) · [Uncovering PA coverage](https://pabucketlist.com/exploring-the-abandoned-cresson-state-prison-in-cambria-county/)
