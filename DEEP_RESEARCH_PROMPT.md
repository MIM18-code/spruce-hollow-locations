# Deep Research Prompt: Spruce Hollow Location Scouting — Gaps & Unknowns

Use this prompt with a deep research tool (Claude, Perplexity Pro, Gemini Deep Research, etc.) to fill the critical information gaps in the current scouting guide.

---

## The Prompt

```
I am producing a supernatural horror feature film called "Spruce Hollow" (140 pages, 61 shoot days, est. $265K-$290K budget). The story takes place primarily at an abandoned juvenile detention/reform school compound in Western Maryland — a five-building institutional campus with a central yard, administration building, mess hall, dormitories, and an isolation block with concrete cells.

I have 10 candidate locations scouted at the research level. I need you to go deep on the following unanswered questions for each. Provide sourced, current-as-of-2026 answers.

### PRIORITY 1: The Three Best Candidates — Status Verification

**1. Alfred D. Noyes Children's Center (Rockville, MD)**
- What is the CURRENT status as of 2026? It was "temporarily closed" by Maryland DJS in October 2022. Has it reopened? Has there been any announcement about demolition, sale, or redevelopment?
- Are the buildings still standing and intact?
- Has any film or TV production ever shot there?
- What is the specific layout? How many buildings? Is there a central yard? Are there secure/isolation rooms?
- Who specifically at Maryland DJS or Montgomery County would approve a film production permit?
- What is the condition of utilities (power, water, HVAC)?

**2. Pennhurst State School & Hospital (Spring City, PA)**
- Confirm the current private leaseholder/operator and whether they still operate the "Pennhurst Asylum" haunted attraction.
- Has any feature film (not haunted attraction content) shot at Pennhurst in the last 5 years? What was their experience? What did they pay?
- What buildings are accessible vs. structurally condemned? Has there been an asbestos abatement?
- Is there cell or secure room infrastructure anywhere on the 1,400-acre campus, or is it purely hospital wards?
- What is the longest continuous production rental that has been negotiated there?

**3. Trans-Allegheny Lunatic Asylum (Weston, WV)**
- The research mentions a film crew was "publicly thanked" in October 2025. What production was this? Feature? Documentary? What were their rental terms?
- Describe the solitary confinement wing in detail: how many cells, what size, what condition? Are the metal doors intact? Barred windows?
- What is the maximum crew size the facility can support simultaneously?
- Are there restrictions on exterior night shoots (light pollution ordinances, neighbor complaints)?
- What is the West Virginia film tax credit application process and timeline? Any minimum spend threshold?

### PRIORITY 2: Reform School Provenance — The Key Differentiator

**4. Mid-Orange / Warwick State Training School (Warwick, NY)**
- This was listed as a filming location by the Orange County NY Film Office. What specific productions have filmed there? Contact anyone involved.
- What is the current condition of the 1930s-era brick dormitory buildings? Still standing?
- Does the campus have a central yard/quad visible from all buildings?
- What is the Town of Warwick's permitting process and fee structure for film production?
- The research mentions "overnight paranormal events held March 2026." Who operates these? Are they the same entity that would negotiate production access?

**5. Glen Mills Schools (Glen Mills, PA)**
- This is the oldest surviving reform school in the United States (est. 1826). What is the EXACT ownership situation in 2026? The license was revoked in 2019 after abuse documentation. Is there a receiver? A trust? Active litigation that would prevent filming?
- The research mentions "Clock Tower Schools" operating in a portion. What portion? What buildings are vacant?
- The adjacent Sleighton Farm School — what is its current condition? Is the arson damage contained to specific buildings?
- Has ANY production approached Glen Mills or Sleighton Farm for filming? If so, what happened?

### PRIORITY 3: Secondary Locations — Logistics

**6. Green Ridge State Forest (Allegany County, MD)**
- What is the specific MD DNR permit process for commercial film production on state forest land? Fee? Timeline? Restrictions on night shooting?
- Which specific fire roads offer the best "closed canopy" look matching the script's Fire Road 18? (Need GPS coordinates or road names for the scout drive.)
- Are there CCC-era structures still standing in Green Ridge? If so, where?

**7. Rural Gas Stations — Route 40/68 Corridor**
- Identify 3-5 specific operational or recently-closed single-pump gas stations between Hancock and Cumberland, MD with a "converted farmhouse" aesthetic. Need addresses and owner contact info.
- The research mentions the "Flintstone Post Office/Gas Station on US-40 (21600 National Pike)." Confirm this is still standing and get current owner/operator contact.

**8. Motel 6 / Suburban Studios — LaVale, MD**
- Has this specific motel (12310 Winchester Rd SW) been rebranded or closed? Confirm current operator and direct booking contact for a 3-day film production rental.

### PRIORITY 4: Financial & Legal

**9. Maryland Film Tax Credit**
- What is the current FY2026/2027 allocation for the Maryland Film Production Activity Tax Credit? Has the $12M figure changed?
- What is the minimum qualifying spend?
- Can a production split between Maryland (secondary locations) and another state (primary facility) still qualify for the Maryland credit on the Maryland portion?
- Who at the Maryland Film Office handles intake for new productions under $500K?

**10. Pennsylvania Film Tax Credit**
- What is the current PA Film Production Tax Credit rate and cap?
- Is the credit transferable? Refundable?
- Does Pennhurst or SCI Cresson fall within any PA Film Office "priority zone" or enhanced credit area?

**11. West Virginia Film Tax Credit**
- Current rate and application process for the WV Film Industry Investment Act credit?
- Are there minimum spend thresholds? Residency requirements for crew?
- Has TALA or the WV Penitentiary been used as a qualifying location for a credited production?

### PRIORITY 5: Production Logistics at Scale

**12. Crew Base & Housing**
- For a 61-day shoot in Western Maryland / Allegany County: what are the options for housing a crew of 25-30? Extended stay hotels in Cumberland/Frostburg? Monthly rental rates?
- Is there a local film crew pool in the Cumberland/Hagerstown area, or would all crew need to come from Baltimore/DC?
- What catering/craft services companies operate in Western Maryland?

**13. Child Actor Logistics**
- The script requires 4-6 child actors (ages 8-14) for approximately 12 shooting days. What are Maryland's specific regulations for child performers? Hours? Studio teacher requirements? Welfare coordinator?
- Same question for Pennsylvania, West Virginia, New York, and Virginia — in case the primary location is out of state.
- Are there child acting agencies in the Baltimore/DC area that could supply 4-6 kids for period roles?

**14. SFX / VFX Vendors**
- What VFX houses in the Baltimore/DC area handle indie horror work? Budget range $15K-$30K. Need ghost compositing, face morphing, practical augmentation.
- What practical SFX vendors in the Mid-Atlantic can do: compound fracture prosthetics, institutional food effects, blood rigs, children's teeth props?
- Intimacy coordinators who work in the Mid-Atlantic region on indie budgets?

### FORMAT

For each question, provide:
1. **Answer** — direct, factual, sourced
2. **Source** — URL or publication with date
3. **Confidence** — HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW based on source freshness and reliability
4. **Action item** — specific next step (call this person, visit this address, file this application)
```

## Why This Research Is Needed

The current scouting guide is built from publicly available information — Wikipedia, Wikimedia Commons, film office websites, news articles, and urbex documentation. But production decisions require:

- **Current status verification** — buildings get demolished, ownership changes, facilities reopen. Several candidates (Noyes, Pennhurst) could have shifted status since the research was compiled.
- **Interior layout detail** — photos show exteriors but the script needs specific infrastructure (cells, corridors, yards with sightlines). Only an on-site scout or detailed interior documentation can confirm.
- **Rental/access terms** — no publicly available source quotes production rates for any of these facilities. Every one requires a direct conversation.
- **Tax credit mechanics** — the split-state scenario (primary in PA/WV, secondary in MD) creates a complex incentive calculation that needs a direct MFO consultation.
- **Crew logistics at scale** — 61 days in rural Western Maryland is a very different proposition than the 14-18 day estimate in earlier research. Housing, crew sourcing, and vendor availability all need verification.
